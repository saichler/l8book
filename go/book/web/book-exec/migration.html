Part VIII — Migration and Adoption

25. Why Migration Matters

Executives often reject architectural change because they assume it requires a full reset. The original book addresses migration to counter that fear.

The argument is not that everything must change at once.

The argument is that once the hidden cause of drag is understood, companies can begin making architecture explicit progressively.

Migration matters because organizations need a believable path:

  • from ambiguity to clarity
  • from heroic systems to boring systems
  • from rising drag to declining drag
  • from hand-crafted integration to explicit contracts

A credible future requires a practical path from the present.

26. What Non-Technical Readers Should Take Away

A non-technical reader does not need to master the implementation details of Layer 8 to understand its significance.

The important takeaways are these:

  1. Modern software is more expensive than it should be because architecture is often implicit.
  2. Hidden structure turns into coordination, and coordination turns into drag.
  3. That drag is economic before it is technical.
  4. AI does not fix unclear structure; it amplifies it.
  5. Layer 8 proposes explicit architecture as the correction.
  6. Its goal is not incremental productivity improvement.
  7. Its goal is to change the economics of building and operating software systems.
