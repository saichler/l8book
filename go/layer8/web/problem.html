The Problem

Why software slows down,
and why teams can't stop it.

It is not a talent problem. Teams are capable. Tools are abundant. But the systems they build accumulate implicit complexity, and that complexity compounds faster than any team can manage.

The symptoms.

01

Architecture is implicit and fragile.

02

Complexity compounds. Every year is slower than the last.

03

Teams spend more time maintaining correctness than building features.

04

Security, concurrency, and replication are bolted on, not built in.

05

Change is avoided because it amplifies risk.

The failure timeline.

Every company follows the same trajectory.

Building features Maintaining correctness
Year 1 Velocity high. Architecture implicit but tractable.
90% / 10%
Year 2 Coordination appears. Design reviews increase.
70% / 30%
Year 3 Incidents increase. Heroics normalized.
45% / 55%
Year 4 Roadmaps slip. Risk tolerance drops.
25% / 75%
Year 5 Change avoided. Budget defends the past.
10% / 90%

There is another way.

The problem is not inevitable. It is architectural. And architecture can be fixed: by construction, not convention.
